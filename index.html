About Me

I'm a Computer Engineering student at the Georgia Institute of Technology (GPA 3.90/4.00, expected May 2028) on the Distributed Systems and Software Design & Architecture thread. I work across the stack: distributed backends, ML training infrastructure, embedded firmware, PCBs, and the security of the software that runs on top.

As Founder & Distributed Systems Lead of KickBack, a social platform, I architected the backend as 12 bounded microservices (Auth, Social Graph, Feed, Plans, Notifications, and more) in Go with decoupled REST/gRPC contracts. I designed the PostgreSQL data models behind the friend graph and plan lifecycle, enforcing privacy and block/report semantics on every read path, and integrated geospatial indexing (PostGIS, Geohash, H3) with vector similarity search (FAISS, pgvector) to power low-latency recommendations. The client is a cross-platform React Native/TypeScript app with OAuth and JWT refresh-token rotation.

As an ML Systems Researcher on the HiAER-Spike neuromorphic computing project (UC San Diego), I lead memory-efficient training of Spiking Neural Networks in PyTorch/SpikingJelly using surrogate-gradient descent, and engineer hardware-constrained ANN-to-SNN conversion and compression pipelines that fold learned parameters into hardware thresholds. I orchestrate distributed hyperparameter search across GPU clusters with SLURM and Ray Tune, and validate simulator correctness against real neuromorphic fabric deployed via the Neuroscience Gateway.

In ultrasound research at Georgia Tech's Coulter Department of Biomedical Engineering, I implemented phase-conjugation beamforming in MATLAB using Hilbert transforms and complex-conjugate Green's-function weighting. It produced 6-15 dB of focal gain and 30-70% sidelobe suppression under aperture blockage. I also design low-voltage monitoring PCBs in Altium for HyTech Racing.

Alongside all of this, I'm a competitive security player: as a CTF team co-captain I've reverse-engineered 100+ binaries, found and exploited 150+ vulnerabilities, and written 50+ proof-of-concept exploits and remediation tests. I'm drawn to problems where correctness, performance, and adversarial robustness all matter at once.
